Main Task of the PDP

The main purpose of this project was to understand the various methods of completing through the entire design process of a fully functional prototype. Specifically, the task that was needed to be completed was to create a fully automated and functional prototype, within constraints, that would launch a heavy steel sphere into a small container any where up to two metres away from said prototype.

To understand how to create the design and actual prototype, desired objectives of the prototype were first explicitly stated so goals are clearly defined.

A chart of all the functions our design needs to have, and the potential means that can potentially give the design said functions.

Pairwise-comparison (PCC) chart of our design's objectives; ranks the objectives in order to prioritise the importance of objectives for our prototype to achieve.

Best-of-Class Chart used to decide and choose the final prototype design over alternative designs and means. Is done by ranking the designs based on how well they perform in terms of satisfying the desired objectives.

Iterations of the trampoline prototype sub-system of the overall design prototype. From left to right, the pink trampoline was a concept for the idea with string and a small balloon. The balloon idea was attempted on a large scale, but was found to be too unreliable in terms of luanching the sphere accurately and distance-wise. The final iternation of the trampoline sub-system is created from stretchy leggings, whose degree of elasticity allows for the usage of a large bucket for the trampoline.

The elasticity of the leggings-bucket trampoline allows for the sphere to reach great height from just a straight drop from one foot above. Imagine what happens when the trampoline is angled, and the sphere is dropped from two metres above.

Graphics that shows the evolution of the support design of the prototype. The purpose was to make the final prototype design more modular and flexible, but also more secure by directly attaching to the launching mechanism.

Statistics of the final prototype design's perfromance which were collected and calculated. Each stack height (proxy for angle of trampoline) had 20-30 trials done. Calibration curves were also created from the same raw dataset.

The finished construction of the final prototype design. The designed successfully launched the metal sphere into the containment vessel of 15 centimetres in diameter.

One of the major projects of Harvey Mudd's Engineering and Design (E4) Course is to create a fully functional hammer from scratch. The choice of building a hammer is because it requires students to use all the machines in the machine shop, and requires a great amount of detail and attention to successfully fabricate. The hammer is combrised of four parts:

  • Hammer Head
  • Hammer Handle
  • Hammer Soft Face
  • Hammer Hard Face

Every student is given a tool box that has all of the raw materials needed for the fabrication of the hammer.
