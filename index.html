SEP 2016 - PRESENT

MATHEMATICAL MODELLING OF TUBERCULOSIS (TB)

Mathematics Thesis

In Progress

Abstract Mathematical modelling techniques were utilised to model the spread of tuberculosis (TB) within a population. Techniques mainly revolved around the field of differential equations through application of conservation principles and analysis of equilibria. Specifically, differential equation was used through the study of the compartmental Susceptible-Infective-Recovery (SIR) model in order to have a basic understanding of how modelling of diseases within a population works. The SIR model was then changed to account for vaccinations and a latency period in order to align more closely to the actual workings of TB. The new model, called the Susceptible-Exposed/Latent-Infective-Recovery (SEIR/SLIR) model, is therefore derived and analysed to see what determines population equilibria in the presence of TB.
