Why Economics?

Economics is not the study of money, and not about the exchange of money. Building upon that, economics is not analagous to finance. Instead, economics can be thought of as the study of choice. From what lunch you had to deciding what major you want to choose in college, economics is one of the unique fields of academia that can partially, if not fully, explain the effects of all the choices you have made. However, the main reason I was drawn to economics is because it fully utilises mathematics to build the fundamental theorems of economics. This unique combination of social sciences and mathematics is what makes economics so powerful, and so appealing.

Why Mathematics?

Everything can be thought of as a result of mathematics. From the computer you're using to the mechanics of your body that allow you to eat, most, if not all, of such objects and activities can be modelled mathematically. It is this definitive property that draws me to mathematically so profoundly. By realising this fact, and by studying mathematics not as a tool, but as a field, you're further realise that mathematics is not just an area of technicality; it is an art that can help you solve anything you desire.

MATHEMATICAL MODELLING OF TUBERCULOSIS (TB)

Mathematics Thesis

In Progress

Abstract This thesis focuses on understanding the fundamentals of compartmental models in epidemiology and the role that differential equations play within this field of mathematics. Specifically, this thesis goes through the derivation and stability analyses of equilibrium points to systems following variants of the Basic SIR Model. Finally, the core part of this thesis focuses on studying the SLIT Model, a compartmental model developed to modelling and understanding the dynamics of Tuberculosis (TB). It was found through rigorous proof that there exists a disease-free equilibrium point, and unique TB-endemic equilibrium point within the SLIT Model. These results indicate that models based on a simple one-strain TB still provide useful insight in understanding how TB spreads within a population in reality.

Abstract

Vacuum Thermal Evaporation (VTE) is a process that applies heat to solid material (e.g., metal) and allows this material to evaporate and deposite directly onto a substrate. These particles eventually condense and form a thin film of material which can be used in microfabrication. Our client, Professor Brian Bryce from the Engineering Department of Harvey Mudd College, created a high vacuum that implements this process and has tasked our team to create a shutter mechanism that can block metal deposition when closed and expose the inner area of the vacuum tube when opened. In this report, we detail the design process that resulted in our final prototype. Our proposed design is a 3D-printed model of a Slab Shutter that satisfies all our constraints and fulfills our objectives identified in this project. We also present recommendations to improve our current design for future iterations.

Design Alternative: Fan Shutter

This design would incorporate a gear system to move a series of overlapping steel blades from one end of the cylindrical opening to the other end. Each circular blade would be attached to the blade below and above it via a single locking joint, from which the blade would pivor anti-clockwise until concealing the cylindrical opening. The gearing system would be actuated by an external rod found currently on the outside of the vacuum, from which an individual would begin movement of the rod clockwise.

Design Alternative: Leaf Shutter

This design would incorporate a belt system to move a series of interlocking steel blades in a centripetal and centrifugal motion. Mounted to a housing periphery on the rim of the aperture, each blade would slide along a guiding trach via a small rod and take the shape of a circular sector. The belt system would be actuated by rotation of a rod found on the outside of the vacuum. This belt system would then translate kinetic rotational energy to centripetal and centrifugal motion by attaching to a rod located alongside the bottom of the leaf shutter, whih is attached to one of the guiding rods.

It should be noted that this design is from GrabCAD, and that we have no creator rights and ownership over this design whatsoever.

Design Alternative: Slab Shutter

This design is very closely aligned with viewport shutters sold commercially through venders like Kurt J. Lesker. The Slab Shutter features an arch-shaped flapper slab mounted on a tangential axis. This slab is rotated by a belt system, whereby the rotation of the slab in the Z-direciton is actuated by an external rod.

Design Alternative: Heart-Valve Shutter

This design was inspired by mechanical heart valves which are used for patients who have severe valvular disorder. The heart-valve shutter deisng features two semicircular steel slabs which pivot on two different rods. The rotary feed-through would initiate motion of the belt system, thereby opening closing the slabs. The slabs open ninety degrees so that when fully opened they run perpendicular to the periphery of the device.
